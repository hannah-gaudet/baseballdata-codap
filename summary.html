New Vocabulary

A variable is a general name for a values that changes. In baseball, statistics like batting average, strikeout rate, and age are variables. We usually use abbreviations to refer to each of these - writing out the same words over and over can get tiring!

  • Measures of location: Where in space do these statistics fall? Where are the numbers centered?
    • Average/Mean: Calculated by dividing the sum of all measurements by the number of measurements.
    • Mode: The most common measurement in the set.
    • Median: The exact middle of a dataset, half the points fall above and below this point.
  • Measures of spread : How spread out/varied are our samples?
    • Range: How spread out the data is. The difference between the largest and smallest measurement
    • Quartiles: Similar to median, measurements that indicate where the lower and upper 25% of the data fall.
